Direction 1: Card Dashboard

Classic card-based approach with summary stats at top, hive cards below. Each card shows status, location, and quick action button.

  • Pros: Familiar pattern, easy to scan, clear hierarchy
  • Cons: Can get long with many hives
  • Best for: Users who want detail at a glance

Direction 2: Kanban Flow

Horizontal columns showing hive status flow - inspired by your developer background. Hives move through Urgent → Check → Good states.

  • Pros: Visual status at a glance, feels like workflow
  • Cons: Horizontal scroll on mobile, less detail
  • Best for: Quick triage, seeing the big picture

Direction 3: Map Focus

Map-first approach for multi-location users like Elena. Location pins show status, drawer reveals details.

  • Pros: Great for route planning, spatial awareness
  • Cons: Less useful for single-location users
  • Best for: Multi-location beekeepers

Direction 4: List Compact

Dense, efficient list with filter chips. Maximum information density, minimal decoration.

  • Pros: Fast scanning, scales well, filter flexibility
  • Cons: Less visual appeal, harder to spot patterns
  • Best for: Power users with many hives

Direction 5: Task First

Task-oriented approach - leads with "what to do today" rather than hive status. Tasks drive the experience.

  • Pros: Action-oriented, clear daily focus, answers "what's next?"
  • Cons: Hive overview less prominent
  • Best for: Users who want guidance on priorities

Direction 6: Minimal

Ultra-clean, focused on core numbers. Maximum white space, single prominent action.

  • Pros: Calming, focused, modern aesthetic
  • Cons: Less information, requires drill-down
  • Best for: Users who prefer simplicity over detail
